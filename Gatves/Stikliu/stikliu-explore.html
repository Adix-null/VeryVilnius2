STREET OF GLASS

Stiklių gatvė — Glass Street — takes its name from the glaziers and glass merchants who traded here during the Middle Ages. Running through the very heart of Vilnius Old Town, one of the largest surviving medieval old towns in Europe, its cobblestones and narrow courtyards have changed little since the 15th century.

The street is part of the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Vilnius Old Town, recognised for its exceptional density of Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque architecture.

HISTORY

For centuries Vilnius was home to one of the most important Jewish communities in the world, earning the city the title "Jerusalem of Lithuania." The neighbourhood surrounding Stiklių and the nearby Gaono streets, named in honour of the celebrated scholar Vilna Gaon, was the epicentre of that cultural life. During the Second World War these streets formed the Vilnius Ghetto. Today the area is remembered through memorials and the nearby Vilnius Gaon Jewish State Museum.
